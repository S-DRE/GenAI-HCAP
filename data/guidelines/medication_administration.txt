MEDICAL GUIDELINE: MEDICATION ADMINISTRATION IN HOME CARE
=========================================================
Document Reference: HCAP-GL-001
Version: 2.1
Issued: January 2026

---

PURPOSE
-------
This guideline sets out the standards for safe medication administration by home care staff.
It applies to all caregivers operating under the HCAP programme.

---

GENERAL PRINCIPLES
------------------
1. Always verify the patient's identity before administering any medication.
2. Check the medication against the care plan before each administration.
3. Administer medication at the prescribed time. Do not administer early or late by more than
   30 minutes without nurse authorisation.
4. Never crush, split or dissolve tablets unless explicitly instructed in the care plan.
5. Record every administration in the patient's daily medication log immediately after giving it.
6. If a patient refuses medication, do not force them. Record the refusal and contact the
   supervising nurse within one hour.

---

MISSED DOSES
------------
If a dose is missed:
  - Within 1 hour of scheduled time: administer as soon as possible.
  - More than 1 hour late: do NOT administer. Record as missed and contact supervising nurse.
  - Never double up doses to compensate for a missed one.

---

STORAGE
-------
- All medications must be stored in a locked cabinet or box.
- Keep medications away from direct sunlight and moisture.
- Refrigerated medications (e.g. insulin) must be stored at 2–8°C.
- Check expiry dates at every visit. Do not administer expired medication.
- Dispose of unused or expired medication via a pharmacy take-back scheme only.

---

ADVERSE REACTIONS
-----------------
Signs of an adverse reaction include:
  - Rash, hives, or swelling of face/lips/throat (possible allergic reaction)
  - Sudden difficulty breathing
  - Severe vomiting or diarrhoea
  - Loss of consciousness or extreme confusion

If any of these occur: call emergency services (112) immediately.
Do not administer any further medication until emergency services have assessed the patient.
Contact the supervising nurse after calling emergency services.

---

CONTROLLED DRUGS
----------------
Caregivers are NOT authorised to administer controlled drugs (e.g. opioids, benzodiazepines)
unless they hold a specific authorisation documented in the care plan and countersigned by
a registered nurse. When in doubt, do not administer and contact the supervising nurse.

---

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------
For each medication administration, record:
  - Date and time administered
  - Drug name, dose, and route
  - Patient's response (any complaints or reactions)
  - Caregiver signature

Use the HCAP digital log or paper MAR (Medication Administration Record) sheet provided.
