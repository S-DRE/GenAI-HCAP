MEDICAL GUIDELINE: FALL PREVENTION AND RESPONSE IN HOME CARE
=============================================================
Document Reference: HCAP-GL-002
Version: 1.4
Issued: February 2026

---

PURPOSE
-------
This guideline provides caregivers with standards for assessing fall risk, implementing
preventive measures, and responding appropriately when a fall occurs.

---

FALL RISK ASSESSMENT
--------------------
Each patient's care plan contains a fall risk rating: LOW, MEDIUM, or HIGH.

LOW risk:   Patient is fully mobile and independent. Basic precautions apply.
MEDIUM risk: Patient has some mobility limitation or history of falls. Enhanced precautions apply.
HIGH risk:  Patient has significant mobility impairment, recent falls, or neurological condition.
            Maximum precautions apply. Caregiver must remain within earshot at all times.

---

ENVIRONMENTAL PRECAUTIONS (ALL PATIENTS)
-----------------------------------------
- Ensure all walkways are clear of clutter, cables, and loose rugs.
- Non-slip mats must be in place in bathroom, toilet area, and kitchen.
- Adequate lighting in all rooms, especially at night (use nightlights).
- All grab rails must be securely fixed — report any that feel loose immediately.
- Patient's walking aids (frame, stick, wheelchair) must be within reach at all times.
- Footwear must be well-fitting and non-slip. No socks-only walking on smooth floors.

---

ADDITIONAL PRECAUTIONS FOR MEDIUM AND HIGH RISK PATIENTS
---------------------------------------------------------
- Accompany patient during all transfers (bed to chair, chair to toilet, etc.).
- Do not leave patient unattended in the bathroom or on stairs.
- Use transfer belt when assisting with standing if documented in care plan.
- Bed rails should be raised at night if documented in care plan.
- Check that the patient call button or alarm pendant is within reach before leaving.

---

WHAT TO DO IF A FALL OCCURS
----------------------------
STEP 1 — Stay calm. Do not move the patient immediately.
STEP 2 — Assess responsiveness: speak to the patient, check if they are conscious.
STEP 3 — Check for serious injury:
           - Visible deformity (possible fracture): do NOT attempt to move patient.
           - Head injury or loss of consciousness: call emergency services (112) immediately.
           - Complaint of hip, back, or neck pain: do NOT move patient. Call 112.
STEP 4 — If no serious injury is suspected and patient is alert:
           - Help patient into a comfortable position (seated or lying) with support.
           - Do not rush to stand them up — allow a few minutes for composure.
           - Assist patient to chair or bed only when they feel ready and you have support.
STEP 5 — Contact the supervising nurse to report the fall within 30 minutes, regardless of outcome.
STEP 6 — Complete a fall incident report before the end of the visit.

---

AFTER A FALL
------------
- Monitor patient more closely for the next 24 hours for signs of delayed injury.
- Watch for: new pain, bruising, swelling, confusion, reluctance to bear weight.
- Any new symptoms after a fall must be reported to the supervising nurse immediately.
- Review the environment with the patient and family to identify and remove the cause.

---

DOCUMENTATION
-------------
Every fall (or near-miss) must be documented using the HCAP Incident Report Form.
Include: date and time, location, patient's account, environmental factors, injuries noted,
actions taken, and who was contacted.
